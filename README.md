## Rashid Hussain, PhD, RSci, MRSC

Postdoctoral Researcher in Computational Pathology at
Humanitas Research Hospital (IRCCS), Milan, Italy  

Computational chemist and bioinformatician working at the interface of molecular modelling, spatial omics, and machine learning. My research focuses on developing reproducible computational frameworks for integrating multi-omics and bioimage data to understand tumor microenvironment dynamics and therapeutic response. ### Research Interests

- Spatial omics and Imaging Mass Cytometry (IMC)  
- Tumor microenvironment modelling and biomarker discovery  
- Computational drug discovery and molecular modelling  
- Machine learning for bioactivity prediction and multi-omics integration  
- Reproducible and scalable computational pipelines  

---

### Technical Expertise

- **Programming:** Python, R, Bash  
- **Bioinformatics:** RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, scRNA-seq, genome annotation  
- **Spatial & Imaging:** IMC workflows, SpatialExperiment
- **Computational Chemistry:** Molecular docking, molecular dynamics (GROMACS, AMBER), free energy profiling  
- **Machine Learning:** QSAR modelling, SVM, Random Forest, ensemble methods  
- **Workflow Development:** Reproducible pipelines using Nextflow and automation tools

### Current Research

- Development of the **Sarcoma Microenvironment Score (SMS)**, an AI-based predictive and prognostic framework  
- Design of scalable pipelines for microenvironment feature extraction and multi-modal data integration  
- Spatial modelling of drug distribution and tissue architecture
